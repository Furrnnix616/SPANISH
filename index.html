ECONOMIC GROWTH

México it is an economy with special strength in the service sector. In 2018, this sector represented 66% of GDP and 62% of the labor force, followed by the industrial sector with 31% of GDP and 23.8% of the labor force, and the primary sector with 2.8% of GDP and 13.6% of the workforce. [1] The Mexican economy has been growing at an average rate of 2.2% during the last 10 years despite the complex recent global environment. It is expected that the Mexican economy will continue to grow in 2019 and 2020. The economic development strategy of the Government of Mexico will strengthen this growth, which includes the strengthening of consumption due to the effect of social programs and greater financial inclusion, the promotion of private investment in infrastructure and strategic sectors, greater public investment and the increase in exports as a result of the reconfiguration of global value chains .

ECONOMIC GROWTH

Vietnam It has 92 million inhabitants, is the 13th most populated in the world, and its economic growth in the last decade is among the largest in the world. With an extension of 331 thousand km2 and a GDP per capita of 1, 550 dollars, it is a country with a socialist political regime, but since 1986 adopted a series of economic reforms that have made it export a lot, receive a lot of foreign investment, and have reduced their level of poverty in important terms. DIt is from its 1986 reforms, its policy of Doi Moi (Renewal), Vietnam has grown at an average annual rate of around 7%. As a result of that policy, it is now one of the largest oil producers in Asia, and one of the world's largest exporters of coffee, rice and the footwear industry. It also exports clothes, electronic products, sea products, chestnut nuts, etc. Its biggest market is the USA, then China, Japan, etc. On the side of imports, its biggest partners are China, South Korea, Japan, Singapore, Thailand and Asian countries.

ACADEMIC DEVELOPMENT

Vietnam managed to get ahead and overcome many of the consequences of the war, although it should be noted that the traces left in its population by so many years of conflict still continue. Today, Vietnam is unified and has internal stability. It is the thirteenth most populous country in the world, with a large fluid majority in Vietnamese, the official language of the territory. It also has a small percentage of the French-speaking population and a growing number of people who are fluent in the English language [1]. The education system in Vietnam starts when children are between 5 and 7 years old. As in many other countries, kindergarten is not mandatory although it is an option that many parents choose. Then, they follow the stages of primary (5 years of duration), middle school (4 years) and high school (3 years). On average, Vietnamese finish school between the ages of 17 and 19. The university in Vietnam lasts between 4 and 6 years, depending on the chosen career. Some of the outstanding universities in the country are: Hanoi University of Science and Technology, National University of Ho Chi Minh City.
